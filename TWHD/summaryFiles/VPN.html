VPN and VPN Chains

General Information

A Virtual Private Network (VPN) extends a private network across a public network, such as the internet. It enables users to send and receive data securely over a shared or public network as if their computing devices were directly connected to the private network.

How It Works

A VPN creates a secure tunnel between the user's device and the VPN server. All data traffic sent over this tunnel is encrypted, ensuring privacy and confidentiality. VPN chains involve routing data through multiple VPN servers to further enhance anonymity.

How to Install and Configure

To use a VPN, you typically need to subscribe to a VPN service and install their software or configure built-in VPN settings on your device. Configuration involves specifying the VPN server's address, authentication credentials, and encryption settings.

How to Use

Once installed and configured, you can connect to the VPN server using the provided client software or device settings. Once connected, all internet traffic will be routed through the VPN server, providing security and privacy.
