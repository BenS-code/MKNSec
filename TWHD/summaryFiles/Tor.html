Tor Browsing

General Information

The Onion Router (Tor) is a free and open-source software that enables anonymous communication by directing internet traffic through a worldwide volunteer network consisting of thousands of relays to conceal a user's location and usage from anyone conducting network surveillance or traffic analysis.

How It Works

Tor works by encrypting data multiple times and routing it through a series of relays, also known as nodes, before reaching its destination. Each relay decrypts a layer of encryption to reveal the next relay in the circuit. This process ensures that no single relay knows both the source and destination of the data, enhancing anonymity.

How to Install and Configure

To use Tor, you can download and install the Tor Browser Bundle, which includes a modified version of the Firefox browser configured to use Tor. Alternatively, you can configure other web browsers to use the Tor network by specifying the Tor proxy settings.

How to Use

Once installed, simply launch the Tor Browser and start browsing. Tor will automatically route your internet traffic through the Tor network, providing anonymity and privacy. It's essential to understand that while Tor enhances privacy, it may not guarantee complete anonymity and may be subject to certain risks.
